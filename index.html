Yily : The Adventure Finder App

UX and UI Design Case Study

See Prototype

Project Overview

Yily is an adventure finder app aims to create a comprehensive platform for adventure seekers, streamlining the process of planning and participating in thrilling activities.

Constraints

4 Week timeline, iOS platform, mobile first design

Problem Space

I designed the adventure finder app to address the growing demand for unique and personalized travel experiences among modern adventurers who seek more than just the typical tourist attractions. With an overwhelming number of options and a lack of reliable, curated resources, travelers often struggle to discover off-the beaten-path destinations and activities that align with their interests and adventure level.

User Interview Findings

Financial Aspects

Affordable travel: Highlighting cost-effective travel options for budget-conscious travelers.

Security

Address concerns by guiding travelers on how to identify and avoid scams during bookings.

Challenges

Acknowledge the struggle of finding consistent and reliable information across various websites.

How Might We

How might we enhance the experience of adventure seekers by offering detailed adventure plans and real time journey updates?

Task Flow

Yily begins with user onboarding, where new users sign up, set up their profiles, and specify their adventure preferences. Users discover adventures through search and filters, accessing detailed information on itineraries, maps, and reviews. They can customize their adventure plans, generate packing lists, and review safety checklists. .

Ideation Sketches

After establishing my task flow, I started sketching some ideation sketches of what the application could look like when translated digitally. I made multiple versions, shown below is the chosen sketch that I based my wireframes on.

Testing

After my initial wireframes were complete, as part of the design process, I conducted rounds of usability testing with 5 users in order to obtain practical, real-time feedback to improve the design and provide a more optimal user experience. Some of the high priority suggestions are the following; Filter & Sorting of adventures: Pricing, adventure level type. Search Location: Location of the hotel to the meeting point. Contact information of the tour guide .Real time Weather updates. Emergency contact around the area.

Learning Outcomes

Research is super essential in UX design. To design a solution that truly benefits the user, it’s essential to create a well-thought research plan and execute it to uncover hidden pain points. The continuous iteration of work can feel like an endless process, becoming tiring at times and causing confusion about the next steps. In moments, of uncertainty, it’s important to remind yourself of the north star and consistently prioritize the needs of your persona.
